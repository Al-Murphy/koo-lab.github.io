The Koo Laboratory studies the functional impact of genomic variants through a computational lens using deep learning. We are broadly interested in gene regulation, RNA-protein interactions, and protein structure. Our approach develops methods to interpret high-performing deep learning models to distill knowledge that they learn from big, noisy, biological sequence data. Our higher-level goal is to elucidate data-driven insights into the underlying biological mechanisms for sequence-function relationships, which can help to prioritize genomic mutations associated with complex diseases, including cancer.
