The Koo Laboratory studies the functional impact of genomic variants through a computational lens using data-driven deep learning solutions. We are broadly interested in applications for gene regulation and protein structure. Our approach develops methods to interpret high-performing deep learning models to distill knowledge that they learn from big, noisy, biological sequence data. Our goal is to elucidate the biological mechanisms that underly sequence-function relationships. More broadly, this can help to prioritize genomic mutations associated with complex diseases, including cancer.
